Orion Financial

Website Redesign

Overview

Orion Financial needed a redesigned website that reflected its evolution into an innovative, user-focused financial partner. The goal was to create a digital experience that felt easy to use, modern, and interactive, while balancing the trustworthiness expected from a financial institution. As Lead Project Designer and Manager, I guided the UX/UI strategy, design process, and cross-functional collaboration.

The Problem

The previous website suffered from outdated design with text-heavy pages and minimal interactivity, confusing navigation making it hard to separate Personal vs. Business services, low discoverability of key products, and a lack of engagement — the site felt formal, static, and uninspiring.

Goals

Build a clean, intuitive layout with logical content hierarchy

Use modern UI patterns and micro-interactions for a forward-thinking brand

Create distinct pathways for Personal and Business banking

Design a more dynamic and visually rich user experience

Incorporate playful copy and approachable visuals without compromising trust

Process

1. Discovery & Research

Conducted stakeholder interviews, analyzed user feedback and pain points, and performed competitive analysis of modern banking and fintech websites.

2. Information Architecture

Created site maps to reorganize content into two clear categories: Personal and Business. Tested navigation prototypes with users to confirm discoverability.

3. Wireframing & Layout

Developed low-fidelity wireframes to map task flows. Prioritized modular content blocks with bold CTAs and designed layouts to support scannability.

4. Visual Design & Interactivity

Introduced modern UI patterns including card layouts, hover states, responsive grids, and micro-interactions. Applied a fresh color palette with playful microcopy.

5. Accessibility & Testing

Ensured WCAG 2.0 compliance with contrast ratios, alt-text, and keyboard navigability. Conducted usability testing sessions on interactive prototypes.

Challenges

• Balancing fun and innovative design while remaining credible in a financial context

• Ensuring interactivity didn't compromise usability for all users

• Aligning playful copy with clear financial product information

Outcomes

Streamlined navigation and modular layouts reduced confusion

Interactive elements modernized the digital presence

Personal vs. Business separation made services easier to find

Approachable visuals and conversational tone encouraged deeper exploration

Orion now feels approachable, modern, and innovative
